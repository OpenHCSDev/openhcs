\begin{abstract}
We study exact consistency when one latent fact is represented at multiple locations that may be modified over time. Once an observer's transcript leaves a nontrivial ambiguity class, exact recovery becomes a zero-error information problem.

We formulate the problem as a zero-error resolvability model for modifiable multi-location encodings. The model separates independent locations from derived ones and measures rate by the number of independently writable degrees of freedom. We prove that zero-incoherence capacity is exactly $1$: exact consistency is achievable at rate $1$ and impossible at any higher independent rate. We then prove a unified finite converse: a $k$-way ambiguity class requires at least $\log_2 k$ bits of zero-error side information, and the same finite budget yields conditional-entropy/Fano bounds for the latent source and decoder output, together with KL-based output-gap witnesses.

The result is a single deterministic finite theorem program with counting, confusability, entropy, decoder-output, and PMF/KL forms. Applications are corollaries; core theorem chains are machine-checked in Lean 4.

\textbf{Index Terms:} zero-error capacity, side information, multi-terminal source coding, exact consistency, confusability graph
\end{abstract}
