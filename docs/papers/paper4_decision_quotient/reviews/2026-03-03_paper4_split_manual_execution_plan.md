# Paper 4 Split Plan (Manual, Dependency-Ordered)

## Objective

Execute a three-paper split of Paper 4 with the dependency order:

1. **Paper A (first, career paper): Foundations + Complexity**
2. **Paper B (second): Foundations + Convergence**
3. **Paper C (third): Physical Grounding + Engineering Consequences**

Design rule from reviewer guidance:

- A depends only on shared foundations.
- B depends only on shared foundations.
- C depends on both A and B.
- A and B must be publication-independent except for shared foundations.

This is an execution checklist for manual operation in small, reviewable passes.

5. `paper4b` currently points to `proofs_4b`; this must be replaced with shared `proofs`.

## Dependency DAG and Citation Contract

### Theoretical DAG

- **Shared foundation layer**: coimage/universal property/sufficiency primitives.
- **A-only layer**: complexity dichotomy, hardness classes, tractability boundaries.
- **B-only layer**: convergence/Bayesian-optimality/information-geometry chain.
- **C-only layer**: physical transport, calibration, engineering corollaries.

### Writing Contract

- A: self-contained definitions + complexity theorem stack, no reliance on B.
- B: self-contained definitions + convergence theorem stack, no reliance on A.
- C: may cite A/B results as prior theorems; should not re-prove full A/B stacks.

### Strategic Boundary (Hard Rule)

- Paper A must be fully non-physical in theorem content, assumptions, and primary narrative.
- Paper B must be fully non-physical in theorem content, assumptions, and primary narrative.
- Paper C is the only downstream paper allowed to carry substantive physical grounding, thermodynamic assumptions, calibration/transport arguments, stochastic/sequential physical-regime narrative, or engineering-reporting doctrine.
- A and B may mention physical motivation only in brief contextual prose; they may not rely on physical premises, physical bridge theorems, or physics-facing examples for theorem completion.
- Any content that would reasonably cause a reviewer to read A or B as making a physical-science claim belongs in C or remains `paper4_toc`-only.

### Canonical Decomposition Contract

Use `paper4_toc` as the canonical source-bank for terminology and theorem inventory, then copy only the needed parts into each downstream paper.

#### Shared foundations that A and B must each restate

- optimizer quotient / coarsest action-preserving abstraction
- coordinate sufficiency / relevance primitives
- decision-question vs encoded-decision-problem distinction
- encoding-regime distinctions: explicit-state vs succinct vs query access
- `srank` / relevant-coordinate cardinality terminology where needed
- theorem naming and claim-language consistency for shared objects

#### Paper A must absorb from `paper4_toc`

- problem-first framing from the TOC introduction, rather than a proof-assistant-first framing
- the core terminology needed to define decision-relevant information without referring to B or C
- the foundations material needed to state complexity results cleanly:
  - optimizer quotient
  - sufficiency/relevance
  - encoding contract
  - abstract encoding-scoped interpretation of complexity claims
- the complexity-facing pieces of the omnibus story:
  - `SUFFICIENCY-CHECK` coNP-completeness
  - `MINIMUM-SUFFICIENT-SET` hardness statements
  - `ANCHOR-SUFFICIENCY` `Sigma_2^P`-completeness
  - tractable subclasses
  - ETH-conditioned lower bounds

#### Paper A must not remain responsible for

- full Bayesian-optimality derivation as a central narrative
- six-framework `srank` recovery as a central narrative
- stochastic/sequential regime narratives or regime-hierarchy narratives
- physical transport / thermodynamic lift / calibration chains
- engineering-reporting doctrine, measurement protocol, or architecture-heavy consequences
- proof-engineering / experiments / discussion sections that are not required for A's independent theorem arc

#### Paper B owns

- concise restatement of shared foundations for independent readability
- coimage / universal-property viewpoint as a central narrative
- convergence framework synthesis
- six-framework `srank` recovery story
- Bayesian-optimality chain, including the `log x <= x - 1` derivation path
- any information-geometry or convergence interpretation that is not needed to state A's complexity theorems
- no physical premises, no thermodynamic premises, and no physics-facing bridge claims

#### Paper C owns

- physical grounding sections
- stochastic/sequential physical-regime narrative and regime hierarchy narrative
- transport / calibration / substrate-cost synthesis
- temporal-learning / temporal-integrity / cross-regime engineering synthesis
- engineering consequences and reporting/integrity corollaries
- explicit dependence on A for the complexity backbone and on B for convergence/Bayesian backbone

### Statement Distribution Ledger (Mandatory)

Create and maintain a ledger table before and during the split. Minimum columns:

- statement label
- statement type (`definition`, `lemma`, `proposition`, `corollary`, `theorem`, `remark`)
- short claim name
- current TOC file
- Lean handle(s), if any
- dependency class:
  - `shared-foundation`
  - `A-only`
  - `B-only`
  - `physical`
- downstream owner:
  - `A`
  - `B`
  - `C`
  - `TOC-only`
- downstream treatment:
  - `full statement`
  - `shortened recap`
  - `cite only`
  - `retire from downstream papers`
- notes on rewrites / copied wording / citation target

Hard rules:

- Every substantive theorem / lemma / proposition / corollary / definition in `paper4_toc` must appear in the ledger.
- Every ledger item must have exactly one downstream owner or be marked `TOC-only`.
- Any item with dependency class `physical` must be owned by `C` or marked `TOC-only`.
- A and B may cite physical items only historically; they may not use them for theorem completion.
- The split is not complete until the ledger proves there is no silent loss of important TOC content.
