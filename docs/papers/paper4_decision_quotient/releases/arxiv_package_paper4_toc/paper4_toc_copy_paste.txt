Title: Computational Complexity of Physical Counting

Abstract (Unicode, for Zenodo):
Abstract

Which coordinates of a system’s state determine the optimal action? For decision problem 𝒟 = (A, S, U) with S = X₁ × ⋯ × Xₙ, set I is sufficient if s_I = s′_(I) ⇒ Opt (s) = Opt (s′). The optimizer quotient Q = S/∼ is the coarsest abstraction preserving optimal actions.

From counting measure we derive probability, Bayes’ theorem, and Bayesian optimality (from log x ≤ x − 1 alone). The invariant srank (relevant-coordinate cardinality) emerges as the complexity measure.

Complexity: SUFFICIENCY-CHECK and MINIMUM-SUFFICIENT-SET are coNP-complete; ANCHOR-SUFFICIENCY is Σ₂ᴾ-complete; stochastic/sequential variants PP-/PSPACE-complete. Six subcases admit polynomial algorithms. Under ETH, 2^Ω(n) lower bounds apply.

Thermodynamic results use Landauer’s floor k_BTln 2 as a declared premise; mismatch/residual dissipation terms yield dU ≥ λ dC. Reduction correctness is machine-checked in Lean 4; polytime computability is a standard hypothesis.

Abstract (MathJax, for arXiv):
Abstract

Which coordinates of a system's state determine the optimal action? For decision problem $\mathcal{D}=(A,S,U)$ with $S=X_1\times\cdots\times X_n$, set $I$ is sufficient if $s_I=s'_I\Rightarrow\operatorname{Opt}(s)=\operatorname{Opt}(s')$. The optimizer quotient $Q=S/{\sim}$ is the coarsest abstraction preserving optimal actions.

From counting measure we derive probability, Bayes' theorem, and Bayesian optimality (from $\log x\leq x-1$ alone). The invariant $\mathrm{srank}$ (relevant-coordinate cardinality) emerges as the complexity measure.

Complexity: SUFFICIENCY-CHECK and MINIMUM-SUFFICIENT-SET are coNP-complete; ANCHOR-SUFFICIENCY is $\Sigma_2^P$-complete; stochastic/sequential variants PP-/PSPACE-complete. Six subcases admit polynomial algorithms. Under ETH, $2^{\Omega(n)}$ lower bounds apply.

Thermodynamic results use Landauer's floor $k_BT\ln 2$ as a declared premise; mismatch/residual dissipation terms yield $dU\geq\lambda\,dC$. Reduction correctness is machine-checked in Lean 4; polytime computability is a standard hypothesis.
